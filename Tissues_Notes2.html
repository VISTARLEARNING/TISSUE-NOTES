Types of Tissues

ðŸŒ±Meristematic Tissue

  • Characteristics: Composed of actively dividing cells.
  • Types:
    • Apical Meristem: Located at the tips of roots and shoots; contributes to lengthening.
    • Lateral Meristem: Found along the sides of stems and roots; responsible for growth in thickness.
    • Intercalary Meristem: Present at nodes; contributes to growth in certain plants.

ðŸŒ¿Permanent Tissue

  • Formation: Derived from meristematic tissue after differentiation.
  • Types:
    • Simple Permanent Tissue:
      • Parenchyma: Loosely packed cells, stores food; can contain chlorophyll (chlorenchyma) or air cavities (aerenchyma).
      • Collenchyma: Provides flexibility; located below the epidermis.
      • Sclerenchyma: Provides rigidity and support; cells are dead with thickened walls.
    • Complex Permanent Tissue:
      • Xylem: Transports water and minerals; consists of tracheids, vessels, xylem parenchyma, and fibers.
      • Phloem: Transports food; composed of sieve tubes, companion cells, phloem parenchyma, and fibers.

ðŸ§¬Epithelial Tissue

  • Types:
    • Squamous Epithelium: Thin, flat cells; found in linings such as blood vessels.
    • Cuboidal Epithelium: Cube-shaped cells; found in kidney tubules.
    • Columnar Epithelium: Tall cells; involved in absorption and secretion.
    • Ciliated Epithelium: Columnar cells with cilia; found in the respiratory tract.
    • Glandular Epithelium: Specialized for secretion.

ðŸ§©Connective Tissue

  • Types:
    • Blood: Fluid matrix with cells suspended; transports materials.
    • Bone: Hard matrix containing calcium; provides structural support.
    • Cartilage: Flexible; smoothens joint surfaces.
    • Areolar Tissue: Fills space in organs, supports, and repairs tissue.
    • Adipose Tissue: Stores fat and acts as an insulator.

ðŸ’ªMuscular Tissue

  • Types:
    • Striated Muscle: Voluntary, attached to bones, has striations.
    • Smooth Muscle: Involuntary, found in organs, no striations.
    • Cardiac Muscle: Involuntary, found in the heart, striated.

ðŸ§ Nervous Tissue

  • Function: Transmits nerve impulses for coordination.
  • Structure: Consists of neurons with a cell body, axon, and dendrites.
